Blog Post: Net Neutrality

Posted on 08/08/2017.

  • HOME
  • APPLICATION AND CONSEQUENCES
  • POLITICS AND ETHICS
  • MATHEMATICS
  • BLOG

As boats and planes allowed people of differing cultures to meet each other physically, the internet allows mental and artistic connection between society, creating globalized collaboration. But with the invention of boats comes devastating plagues, and with the internet, there are many. An entirely new breed of criminal has arisen - one that can be completely anonymous, and economically devastating. Privacy is also under threat - social media sites let complete strangers know more about you now then a family member would 200 years ago. The government has more access to people's private accounts than ever before. We’re already beginning to see examples of this in the news: Russian hackers, email scandals, net neutrality, this threat to security and privacy is very real, but is also misrepresented. Such an important topic shouldn’t be and isn’t scary to understand - it’s just made that way by politicians and the media.

We’re here not to fear-monger, but to explain in terms all can understand.

We’re here to decypher cybersecurity.
